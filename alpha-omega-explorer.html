BR-split with gated allocations

How it works
α Alpha
• Determines the service budget: what leaves consensus each epoch.
• Ramps up continuously every epoch from 5% to 50% over 9 quarters (~5 pp / quarter). Deterministic, time-based — no revenue gate.
αfloor Alpha_floor
• The allocation rate: the fraction of block rewards allocated to the Service Orchestrator.
• Revenue-gated. Starts at 10% after Q1 (bootstrap quarter, ungated). From Q2 onward, steps up by 5 pp per quarter if on-chain deal revenue clears the target; otherwise holds.
burn α − αfloor = burned. If αfloor tracks α, minimal burn. If αfloor lags, the gap burns (deflationary).
Per-epoch split
Consensus: (1−α) × BR  |  Allocation: αfloor × BR  |  Burn: (α − αfloor) × BR
Revenue gate
MeasuredDealRevenue(Q) ≥ β × Allocation(Q−1)  |  pass → αfloor += step_size, else hold
β Beta (target multiplier)
• Sets how much deal revenue must exceed last quarter’s allocation to unlock the next αfloor step. Starts low (βstart) and escalates toward βmax as αfloor grows, making later steps progressively harder to clear.
More info: FIL+ Reform – Alpha Ramp-Up Design Doc
How to use this explorer
Step 1: Choose a revenue growth model below. Deal revenue = total $ value of paid storage deals (not the 0.5% network fee). Adjust the starting revenue, growth rate, and FIL price to match your assumptions.
Step 2: Tune the gate parameters (β start, β max, k shape) to see how they affect αfloor progression and burn. The α ramp (5% → 50% over 9Q) and αfloor step size (5 pp) are fixed.
Step 3: Check the simulated outputs below — the quarterly scenario table and charts update live as you move the sliders.

How these models work ↓

Expected deal revenue in Q2 (RQ2) is the first measured quarter — total $ value of paid storage deals on-chain (Q1 is the bootstrap quarter with no revenue measurement). Growth rate (g) compounds from Q2 forward using one of these models:

Decaying growth: R(i) = R(i−1) × (1 + gi) where gi = floor + (g − floor) × e−0.45(i−1)
Growth rate starts at g (slider value) in Q2, decays exponentially toward the long-term rate steady state.

Peak-and-settle: R(i) = R(i−1) × (1 + gi), gamma-shaped growth rate normalised so g1 = g (slider value)
Starts at g, ramps up over 2–3 quarters (peak ~Q4), then steep decay to floor by ~Q13. As revenue gets large, growth rate drops fast.

Wasabi benchmark: Implied QoQ growth rates derived from Wasabi Technologies (cloud storage, founded 2017). Verified annual revenue: $18M→$54M→$92.5M→$134.7M→$160.1M (2020–2024). Sources: getlatka.com, blocksandfiles.com, wasabi.com press releases.
Each year’s QoQ = (end/start)1/4−1, held constant across 4 quarters: 32%/q → 14%/q → 10%/q → 4%/q. Growth rate and long-term rate sliders are ignored. Starting deal revenue slider still sets R0 (Wasabi’s absolute $ figures don’t apply to Filecoin — only the growth shape is borrowed).

Block reward: 63,727 FIL/day (Apr 2026, via Filfox) with 6-year half-life decay. BRq = start-of-quarter daily rate × 90 days × FIL price (~1.4% overestimate vs exact integration).

Note: This model only applies simple minting decay to BRq. It does not account for baseline minting, which depends on network QAP vs the baseline power function. Currently baseline contributes ~14% of daily emission because QAP is well below baseline. If QAP were to grow, baseline minting would increase BRq beyond what this model projects, raising the gate target.
