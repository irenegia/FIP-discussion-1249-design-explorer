Block-reward split

Each epoch the block reward is divided across two streams by weight. The weights always sum to 1 (100%).

How it works
w0 Consensus weight (w0)
• The existing consensus network will receive w0 × BR each epoch — paid to the winning miner exactly as today.
• Follows a policy trajectory: starts at 95% and ramps down continuously to ~50% over 9 quarters (~5 pp / quarter). Deterministic and time-based — no revenue gate.
w1 Service-subnet weight (w1)
• A new incentive stream for storage providers delivering a service. It receives w1 × BR, split among orchestrators by their verifiable revenue.
• Revenue-gated. Starts at 10% after Q1 (bootstrap quarter, ungated). From Q2 onward, steps up by 5 pp per quarter if on-chain deal revenue clears the target; otherwise holds.
wb Burn weight. The remainder is burned each epoch: wb = 1 − ∑i wi = 1 − w0 − w1. If w1 tracks the share that leaves consensus (1−w0), minimal burn. If w1 lags, the gap burns (deflationary).
Per-epoch split — weights sum to 1
Consensus: w0 × BR  |  Service subnet: w1 × BR  |  Burn: wb × BR   (w0 + w1 + wb = 1)
Revenue gate (drives w1)
MeasuredDealRevenue(Q) ≥ β × ServiceAlloc(Q−1)  |  pass → w1 += step_size, else hold
β Beta (target multiplier)
• Sets how much deal revenue must exceed last quarter’s service allocation to unlock the next w1 step. Starts low (βstart) and escalates toward βmax as w1 grows, making later steps progressively harder to clear.

How to use this explorer
Step 1: Choose a revenue growth model below. Deal revenue = total $ value of paid storage deals (not the 0.5% network fee). Adjust the starting revenue, growth rate, and FIL price to match your assumptions.
Step 2: Pick the escalation shape (power-law or exponential) and tune the gate parameters (β start, plus k / β max, or the per-step ratio) to see how they affect the w1 progression and burn. The w0 trajectory (95% → 50% over 9Q) and the w1 step size (5 pp) are fixed.
Step 3: Check the simulated outputs below — the quarterly scenario table and charts update live as you move the sliders.
⚠ Block-reward overestimate: This model applies simple-minting decay (6-year half-life) to the entire block reward. In reality, ~14% of current emission comes from baseline minting, which decays much faster when QAP declines (currently −30 PiB/day). Result: the model overestimates BRq by roughly +1% in Q1, growing to ~+14.5% by Q16 (average ≈ +9% over 16 quarters). This makes the revenue gate harder to clear than it would be in practice — a conservative/pessimistic bias. Keep this in mind when interpreting near-pass or near-fail scenarios in later quarters.

How these models work ↓

Expected deal revenue in Q2 (RQ2) is the first measured quarter — total $ value of paid storage deals on-chain (Q1 is the bootstrap quarter with no revenue measurement). Growth rate (g) compounds from Q2 forward using one of these models:

Decaying growth: R(i) = R(i−1) × (1 + gi) where gi = floor + (g − floor) × e−0.45(i−1)
Growth rate starts at g (slider value) in Q2, decays exponentially toward the long-term rate steady state.

Peak-and-settle: R(i) = R(i−1) × (1 + gi), gamma-shaped growth rate normalised so g1 = g (slider value)
Starts at g, ramps up over 2–3 quarters (peak ~Q4), then steep decay to floor by ~Q13. As revenue gets large, growth rate drops fast.

Custom (per-quarter): You set the QoQ growth rate individually for each quarter from Q3 to Q16. The Q2 starting revenue slider sets RQ2; after that, each quarter compounds at the rate you type in. Growth rate and long-term rate sliders are replaced by the per-quarter inputs.

Wasabi benchmark: Implied QoQ growth rates derived from Wasabi Technologies (cloud storage, founded 2017). Verified annual revenue: $18M→$54M→$92.5M→$134.7M→$160.1M (2020–2024). Sources: getlatka.com, blocksandfiles.com, wasabi.com press releases.
Each year’s QoQ = (end/start)1/4−1, held constant across 4 quarters: 32%/q → 14%/q → 10%/q → 4%/q. Growth rate and long-term rate sliders are ignored. Starting deal revenue slider still sets R0 (Wasabi’s absolute $ figures don’t apply to Filecoin — only the growth shape is borrowed).

Block reward: 63,727 FIL/day (Apr 2026, via Filfox) with 6-year half-life decay. BRq = start-of-quarter daily rate × 90 days × FIL price (~1.4% overestimate vs exact integration).

Note: This model only applies simple minting decay to BRq. It does not account for baseline minting, which depends on network QAP vs the baseline power function. Currently baseline contributes ~14% of daily emission because QAP is well below baseline. If QAP were to grow, baseline minting would increase BRq beyond what this model projects, raising the gate target.
